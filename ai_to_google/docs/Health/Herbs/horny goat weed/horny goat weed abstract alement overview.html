Ailments with Demonstrated Efficacy (Clinical Evidence)

Sorted by the robustness of reported clinical outcomes and effective rates:

  1. Periodontitis (Co-occurring with Osteoporosis): In a retrospective clinical study, Epimedium flavonoid treatment achieved a 91.7% total effective rate, significantly improving periodontal health metrics and inflammatory markers in gingival fluid.

  2. Osteoporosis: Clinical trials demonstrate that Epimedium and its extracts significantly increase bone mineral density (BMD) in the lumbar spine and proximal femur, with one study reporting a 95.0% total effective rate as an adjunctive therapy.

  3. Sexual Dysfunction (Erectile Dysfunction): Modern clinical practice and restorative trials show that icariin, the active ingredient in Epimedium, is effective in restoring spontaneous erections and repairing penile tissue pathological changes.

  4. Thin Endometrium: Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) have reported successful application of icariin for increasing endometrial thickness in infertile women, although systematic reviews are ongoing to confirm full scale efficacy.

Ailments with Greatest Theoretical Efficacy (Preclinical/Animal Models)

Sorted by the magnitude of effect observed in laboratory and animal simulations:

  1. Hepatocellular Carcinoma (Liver Cancer): Theoretical synergy between icaritin and autophagy inhibitors shows high efficacy in inducing cancer cell death through PINK1/Parkin-mediated mitophagy.

  2. Ischemic Stroke: In rat models, icariin reduced cortical infarct volume by 32% to 40% and significantly improved neurological function scores by inhibiting mitochondrial permeability transition pore (mPTP) opening.

  3. Colorectal Cancer: Epimedium is theorized to have significant effects when combined with PD-1 inhibitors, as it promotes ferroptosis and activates CD8(+) T cells to secrete IFN-γ, enhancing immunotherapy response.

  4. Inflammatory Diseases (Rheumatoid Arthritis & Ulcerative Colitis): High efficacy is predicted for targeted delivery systems (such as exosome-loaded icariin), which have shown marked symptom mitigation, colon lengthening, and M1-to-M2 macrophage phenotypic transitions in animal models.

  5. COVID-19: Research hypothesizes a strong potential for icariin to inhibit SARS-CoV-2 entry into host cells by modulating specific intracellular pathways.

  6. Depression: Network pharmacology and molecular docking technologies predict multi-target mechanisms involving IL6 and AKT1, suggesting a high theoretical capacity for treating depressive symptoms.
